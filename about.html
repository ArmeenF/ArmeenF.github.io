Development

My developing career started because: I love math, problem solving, and being in front of a computer. System.out.println("Hello World!"); was the line of code that would launch me into a new career. It felt exhilarating to have the computer to return that simple phrase, Hello World! I could control what this device did and was anxious to know the extent of what I could make it do. Every time I code an efficient program, learn a new concept, or write an elegant algorithm it fuels my passion for developing. I'm currently a computer science and systems student at the University of Washington. I develop for school and for fun. I am most comfortable with back-end programming and have mainly developed in C# and Java. To practice algorithms and whiteboarding problems I use TheOdinProject and Leetcode. My favorite way of learning a new languages syntax is to build off what others have done.
